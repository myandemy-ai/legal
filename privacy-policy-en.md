# myandemy.ai Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 24, 2026

Myandemy Japan Inc. ("we," "us," or "our") operates myandemy.ai (the "Service"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal information when you use our Service.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with applicable data protection laws, including Japan's Act on the Protection of Personal Information (APPI), the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1 Information You Provide

1. **Account Information**
   - Email address
   - Password (stored in encrypted form)
   - Username and display name (optional)
   - Profile information (optional)

2. **Social Login Information**
   - **Via Google**: Email address, name, and profile picture
   - We only access publicly available profile information from Google. We do not access your password, social media posts, or friend lists.

3. **AI Tutor Input Data**
   - Text data entered when using the AI Tutor feature
   - Voice data submitted when using the AI Tutor feature

4. **Support Inquiries**
   - Content of your inquiries
   - Contact information

### 1.2 Information Collected Automatically

1. **Usage Data**
   - Search history
   - Browsing history
   - Learning history and progress data
   - In-app activity data

2. **Technical Information**
   - IP address
   - Browser type and version
   - Device information (OS, device type, device ID)
   - Access date and time
   - Cookie data

3. **Log Information**
   - Server logs
   - Error logs
   - Access logs

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

1. **Service Provision and Operation**: Account creation and management, login authentication, providing Service features (including the AI Tutor), saving and displaying learning history, and delivering personalized learning experiences.

2. **Service Improvement and Development**: Analyzing usage patterns, developing new features, improving user experience, and fixing bugs.

3. **Communication**: Responding to inquiries, sending important service announcements, and maintenance notifications.

4. **Marketing (Opt-in Only)**: Sending information about the Service, new features, and campaigns. You may opt out at any time.

5. **Security and Fraud Prevention**: Detecting and preventing unauthorized access, investigating violations of our terms, and maintaining system security.

6. **Legal Compliance**: Responding to legal obligations and protecting our rights.

## 3. AI Tutor and Data Sharing with Google

Our AI Tutor feature is powered by Google Gemini API, provided by Google LLC.

### 3.1 Data Sent to Google

When you use the AI Tutor feature, the text and voice data you input are sent to Google's servers to generate AI-powered responses.

### 3.2 How Google Handles Your Data

- We use Google Gemini API as a paid business API. Google does not use the data sent through the API to train its AI models.
- Your data is processed temporarily to generate responses and is handled in accordance with Google's data retention policies.
- For details, please refer to Google's Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy

### 3.3 Precautions When Using AI Tutor

- Do not input sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or government-issued identification numbers into the AI Tutor.
- AI Tutor responses are automatically generated and their accuracy is not guaranteed.

1. When required by law
2. When necessary to protect the life, body, or property of an individual, and it is difficult to obtain consent

4. When cooperating with a government agency or local authority performing duties prescribed by law, and obtaining consent may impede such duties

## 6. Cookies and Similar Technologies

### 6.1 How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

1. **Essential Cookies**: Required for basic Service functionality, such as maintaining login sessions and security features.
2. **Functional Cookies**: Remembering your settings and preferences.
3. **Analytics Cookies**: Analyzing Service usage through tools such as Google Analytics.

### 6.2 Managing Cookie Preferences

You can disable cookies through your browser or device settings. However, disabling cookies may cause some features of the Service to function improperly.

## 7. Security Measures

We implement the following security measures to protect your personal information:

1. **Encryption in Transit**: All communications use SSL/TLS encryption.
2. **Encryption at Rest**: Passwords are hashed before storage. Data stored in our database is encrypted.
3. **Access Control**: Supabase Row Level Security (RLS) ensures that users can only access their own data.
4. **Infrastructure**: Our database platform, Supabase, operates on AWS's secure infrastructure.
5. **Intrusion Prevention**: We use firewalls and intrusion detection systems.
6. **Organizational Measures**: We have appointed a data protection officer and established internal data handling policies.

## 8. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected.

| Data Type | Retention Period |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Account information | Until account deletion, or after 1 year of inactivity |
| Usage history and learning data | Up to 2 years |
| AI Tutor conversation data | Up to 1 year |
| Support inquiries | 1 year after resolution |
| Log information | Up to 6 months |

Where retention is required by law, we will retain information for the legally required period.

Upon account deletion, all personal information associated with your account will be **permanently deleted within 30 days**, except where retention is required by law.

## 10. Rights of EEA and UK Residents (GDPR)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, you have the following additional rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and UK GDPR:

1. **Right to Data Portability**: Receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
2. **Right to Restriction of Processing**: Request restriction of processing of your personal data under certain conditions.
3. **Right to Object**: Object to the processing of your personal data based on our legitimate interests.
4. **Right to Refuse Automated Decision-Making**: Refuse decisions based solely on automated processing that produce legal effects concerning you.

We act as the Data Controller under the GDPR. Our legal bases for processing are: your consent, performance of a contract, our legitimate interests, or compliance with a legal obligation.

To exercise these rights or file a complaint, please contact us using the information below. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection authority.

## 11. Rights of California Residents (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

1. **Right to Know**: Request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected, used, and shared.
2. **Right to Delete**: Request deletion of the personal information we have collected from you.
3. **Right to Opt-Out**: Opt out of the "sale" of your personal information to third parties.
4. **Right to Non-Discrimination**: Not receive discriminatory treatment for exercising your privacy rights.

We do not "sell" your personal information as defined by the CCPA.

To exercise your CCPA rights, please contact us using the information below. You may submit up to 2 requests within a 12-month period, and we will respond within 45 days.

## 12. Children's Privacy
